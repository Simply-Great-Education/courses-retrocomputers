Things you will need for this modification
- philips screwdriver
- flathead screwdriver
- soldering iron
- desoldering gun/pump or solder wick
- a short piece of wire

#### Step 1.

Unscrew the back of the case and lift off the top half of the board.
![1.jpg](https://github.com/Simply-Great-Education/courses-retrocomputers/blob/main/assets/sinclair/spectrum/images/1.jpg)

#### Step 2. 

Lift off the top of the RF modulator with the flathead screwdriver.
![2.jpg](https://github.com/Simply-Great-Education/courses-retrocomputers/blob/main/assets/sinclair/spectrum/images/2.jpg)

#### Step 3.
Desolder the video and power feeds coming out of the left of the RF Modulator.
Then desolder the connection on the resistor going to the RF socket.
![3.jpg](https://github.com/Simply-Great-Education/courses-retrocomputers/blob/main/assets/sinclair/spectrum/images/3.jpg)
![4.jpg](https://github.com/Simply-Great-Education/courses-retrocomputers/blob/main/assets/sinclair/spectrum/images/4.jpg)


#### Step 4.
Feed the new piece of wire through the unused hole on the modulator, and solder it to the top pin of the RF socket.
![5.jpg](https://github.com/Simply-Great-Education/courses-retrocomputers/blob/main/assets/sinclair/spectrum/images/5.jpg)

#### Step 5. 
Reassemble the board, making sure to do it carefully to avoid any unwanted damages.
![6.jpg](https://github.com/Simply-Great-Education/courses-retrocomputers/blob/main/assets/sinclair/spectrum/images/6.jpg)
